# Aspect And Reception Judgment

Use this file when the question turns on 相位, 交角, 接纳, 互容, 定位星, 夹制, 映点, or when a reading is drifting into planet-pair cookbook meanings.

## Core Stance

An aspect is not just "planet A has a keyword relationship with planet B." It is a testimony that one significator acts upon another. Judge it by:

1. Which planet is the significator of the topic.
2. Which planet is the promissor or acting influence.
3. The aspect type and whether it is applying or separating.
4. The dignity, sect, speed, solar condition, and house condition of both planets.
5. Reception, mutual reception, and dispositor condition.
6. The houses ruled and occupied by both planets.

Do not decide event outcomes from aspect type alone. A benefic in poor condition can spoil what it creates; a malefic in good condition can produce durable achievement through difficulty.

## Aspect Types

Prioritize the Ptolemaic aspects:

- Conjunction: union or direct fusion; judge by the planets' condition and nature.
- Sextile: opportunity, assistance, smooth development.
- Square: conflict, friction, labor, obstruction, forced action.
- Trine: ease, support, natural cooperation.
- Opposition: confrontation, separation, polarity, contest, projection.

The classical root of aspect doctrine is sign relationship. First check whether the signs behold each other; then refine by degree, orb, application, and reception. Signs in aversion often show non-contact, ignorance, inability to act together, or matters not naturally seeing each other.

## Orbs And Moiety

Use planetary moiety rather than a fixed orb for each aspect type when exact orb matters:

| Planet | Full orb |
| --- | ---: |
| Sun | 15° |
| Moon | 12° |
| Saturn | 9° |
| Jupiter | 9° |
| Mars | 8° |
| Venus | 7° |
| Mercury | 7° |

Two planets are within operative orb when the distance from exact aspect is within half of planet A's orb plus half of planet B's orb. Example: Moon to Mercury has about 9.5° of combined moiety.

Keep consultation outputs simple: name whether an aspect is tight, moderate, or loose unless the user asks for technical calculation.

## Application And Separation

- Applying aspect: the matter is developing, approaching, or still able to manifest.
- Separating aspect: the matter has already happened, is fading, or belongs to background history.
- Fast planet applying to slow planet often shows the native/topic moving toward the promised condition.
- A planet separating from one testimony and applying to another can show transition from one condition/person/topic to another.

For prediction, applying exactness matters more than a loose natal contact.

## Reception

Reception has direction.

A planet is received when it aspects a planet that rules one of its dignities in the sign it occupies: domicile, exaltation, triplicity, term/bound, or face. In practice, prioritize domicile and exaltation; count triplicity + term + face combinations when a stricter medieval reading is needed.

Reception means the receiving planet has reason or ability to support the received planet. It can soften a square or opposition, but it does not erase the hard aspect's friction. The result may still require labor, conflict, compromise, or payment.

Reception also strengthens connection. If a harmful planet, difficult house lord, or afflicted dispositor receives the topic, the link can make the harmful testimony more effective rather than more fortunate. Ask whether the receiver is capable of helping before calling reception a rescue.

Ask:

- Who receives whom?
- Is the receiver strong enough to help?
- Does the receiver rule or occupy a helpful or harmful house?
- Does the received planet carry the user's topic into the receiver's house topics?

## Mutual Reception

Mutual reception links two planets and their houses strongly. It can exchange resources, bind two life areas, or make two people/topics unable to ignore each other.

Common forms:

- Domicile-to-domicile reception.
- Exaltation-to-exaltation reception.
- Mixed major dignity reception, such as one planet in the other's domicile and the other in the first planet's exaltation.

Do not call mutual reception automatically fortunate. If both planets are cadent, afflicted, or ruling difficult houses, the link may be strong but burdensome. If both have strength and helpful house topics, it can become a major support pattern.

Mutual reception can also bind two problem houses together. In difficult historical cases, reception/aspect did not remove the harm; it gave the harmful planet a route to act on the native or topic. In live consultation, translate this as "strong entanglement / hard to ignore / needs management" unless the biography is already known.

## Dispositor / 定位星

The dispositor of a planet controls the environment through which that planet acts.

- Weak planet with strong dispositor: the matter may begin awkwardly but find support or recovery.
- Strong planet with weak dispositor: the matter may begin well but be undermined by its environment.
- Planet applying to its dispositor: the topic seeks its manager or returns to the source of control.
- Dispositor in a relevant topic house: the planet's promise is routed through that house.

Always inspect the dispositor before finalizing a debilitated planet's judgment.

## Event-Level Aspect Reading

For concrete events, combine three layers:

- Aspect nature: harmonious, difficult, fused, or separating.
- Planet nature: benefic/malefic and natural symbolism.
- Local determination: houses ruled and occupied.

Example logic:

- 2nd lord square Saturn is not merely "financial anxiety." It can show expense, scarcity, delayed payment, blocked income, debt pressure, or conservative accumulation. The concrete form comes from Saturn's house rulership and placement.
- 10th lord trine Jupiter may support career, but if Jupiter rules the 8th and sits in the 12th, the benefit can arrive through institutions, hidden patrons, debt, inheritance, hospitals, research, or behind-the-scenes protection.

Psychological descriptions are allowed, but in classical work they follow from the event structure rather than replace it.

## Conjunctions

Conjunctions are not inherently good or bad. Judge:

- Whether the stronger planet dominates the weaker.
- Whether a malefic harms or disciplines the topic.
- Whether a benefic assists or entices into excess.
- Whether combustion or cazimi changes the condition.
- Whether the conjunction occurs in an angle, topic house, or cadent/hidden place.

Combustion with the Sun weakens ordinary planetary visibility; cazimi is an exception and can empower the planet as if at the heart of authority.

## Besiegement And Enclosure

Besiegement occurs when a planet is caught between malefics or moves from a malefic contact toward another malefic contact. It shows the topic under pressure, with few clean exits.

Enclosure by benefics works as protection, sponsorship, or relief. In practice, look for both degree contact and whole-sign enclosure; use the tighter and more concrete version first.

## Antiscia

Antiscion mirrors a planet across the Cancer-Capricorn solstice axis. Contra-antiscion is the opposition to that mirrored point.

Use antiscia as hidden sympathy, indirect connection, or backstage contact. Use contra-antiscion as hidden conflict or pressure. Do not let antiscia outweigh direct aspect, dignity, or house rulership unless it is exact and repeated elsewhere.

## Nodes

The North Node tends to amplify; the South Node tends to diminish or drain. This rule changes with context:

- North Node with a benefic can enlarge benefit; with a malefic it can enlarge trouble.
- South Node with a malefic can reduce its force; with a benefic it can reduce ease.
- Very close node contacts can act like eclipse or obscuration signatures, so judge carefully.

Use node contacts as modifiers, not as the entire reading.

Node prominence rule: nodes can also spotlight, isolate, or "leak out" a planet in a public/status chart. If a planet is already angular, dignified, or a key house ruler, close node involvement can make it stand out more sharply. This is a prominence modifier, not a replacement for dignity, sect, house rulership, or reception.

## Cookbook Aspect Lists

Planet-pair descriptions are secondary. Use them only after the house and condition logic is known.

When using a planet-pair description:

- Translate it into the houses involved.
- Check whether the aspect is applying or separating.
- Check whether either planet receives the other.
- Check dignity and accidental strength before deciding whether the symbolism succeeds, fails, harms, or merely describes temperament.
