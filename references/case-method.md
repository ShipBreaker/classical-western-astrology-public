# Case Method

## Purpose

Use cases to train judgment, not to copy labels. Public-figure cases are useful for calibration because they combine birth data, biography, status, profession, illness, crime, marriage, death, and dated event notes. The best use is to learn which chart testimonies repeat across a type of life, then test the method against dated events.

## Case Study Workflow

1. Identify the case category: career/status, wealth, art, politics, medicine, crime, illness, marriage, family, spiritual/occult, sports, engineering, etc.
2. Confirm birth data reliability and timezone. If the case comes from Astro-Databank, preserve the Rodden or source quality when available.
3. Build the natal promise:
   - 1st/10th for life direction and status.
   - Topic houses for the event category.
   - Natural significators.
   - Dignity, angularity, sect, aspects, reception.
4. Compare biography:
   - What does the chart explain strongly?
   - What is mixed or ambiguous?
   - Which expected symbols do not appear?
5. Test timing:
   - Use dated events.
   - Compare annual profection, Firdaria/ZR if available, Solar Arc, secondary progressions, and transits.
   - Record converging testimonies and failed testimonies.
6. Extract a reusable rule only when several cases repeat the same structure.

## Pattern Notes

- Public status cases usually require strong 1st/10th/MC/Sun/benefic or patronage testimony, not merely a "good career planet".
- Founder or "father of a field" cases may show not only personal brilliance but also a network pattern: 9th/10th/11th links, students, peers, publishers, schools, or groups carrying the native's work.
- Wealth cases need 2nd/8th/10th/11th links, Fortune or Jupiter/Venus support, and evidence of resource channels.
- Artistic cases can arise from Venus, 5th, 3rd, 10th, Moon, Mercury, and public houses; do not require Venus alone.
- Medical/surgical cases often involve Mars, Saturn, 6th/8th/10th, technical Mercury, and service houses.
- Crime or violence cases require strict caution: malefic signatures must be tied to action, conflict, law, confinement, 8th/12th, and biography. Never apply crime labels to private clients.
- Occult/astrology/spiritual cases often involve 8th/9th/12th, Mercury/Jupiter/Moon, hidden houses, and strong symbolic or teaching indicators.
- A very strong chart can still contain a decisive wound. When biography is known, identify both: where the chart is genuinely excellent and where the specific break, addiction, scandal, illness, isolation, or conflict testimony enters.
- Use "cluster" logic: one planet alone is weaker than a group of connected planets acting through aspect, conjunction, reception, or shared ruler chains. A benefic cluster can gather support toward the Ascendant, MC, or topic ruler; a malefic/8th-house cluster around the Ascendant or body significator can greatly increase health or crisis risk.
- A planet's debility score is not the whole story. A fallen or damaged planet can still become important if it is angular, of sect, received by stronger planets, tied to the MC/Ascendant, or made prominent by nodes. Judge the environment around the planet, not only its dignity label.

## Case Extraction

Use this practical sequence:

- Treat every house judgment as including the house, the house lord, planets in the house, the lord's dignity/debility, placement, aspects, reception/mutual reception, antiscia when relevant, and the houses ruled by the planets involved.
- Start with 1st and 10th for the person's life frame. A strong 1st/10th can lift weak money houses into unstable-but-real resources; weak 1st/10th can reduce a good 2nd house to ordinary support without high status.
- For career, first identify which houses benefit the 1st and 10th, then translate those house links into work content. Use the 6th for daily labor environment, not as the sole career house.
- For wealth, do not stop at the 2nd. Ask whether money comes from personal effort, career rank, clients/partners, family property, patrons/audience, speculation, or institutions.
- For character, do not list every planet separately. Extract common denominators from Ascendant ruler, Sun, Moon, emphasized elements/modes, repeated house clusters, and the contrast between "self" factors and environmental/oppositional factors.
- The chart usually gives a range of viable manifestations, not one exact job title or event. Narrow the range with biography, era, social background, and timing.

## Anti-Overfitting Rules

- Do not treat a famous case as proof that a single aspect causes a life event.
- Do not cherry-pick after knowing the biography. First state what the chart promises, then compare.
- Do not assume a planet belongs to a house mechanically when it is close to the next cusp. Re-check cusp distance, house system, and whether the biography clearly speaks through the following house.
- Do not reverse-engineer every event. Some events are environmental, collective, or family-derived.
- Do not generalize from extreme cases to ordinary consultations.
- Do not ignore missing birth-time reliability; angles can make or break a case.

## Consultation Transfer

When using case learning in a live reading:

- Say "this resembles a pattern of..." rather than "this means you will...".
- Prefer practical language over dramatic labels.
- For sensitive topics, translate harsh symbolism into advice: risk awareness, conflict boundaries, health checkups, communication strategy, financial conservatism, or timing caution.
- If a chart is full of difficult aspects, identify the workable benefic or reception path so the client is not left with only fear. If a chart is very smooth or easy, identify the main weak point so the client does not become careless. This is consultation ethics as much as technique.
