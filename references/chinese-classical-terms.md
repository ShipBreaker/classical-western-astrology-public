# Chinese Classical Astrology Terms

Use this file when reading Chinese classical Western astrology materials such as `天步真原`, 七政-style terminology, 照星/许星, 命宫, 官禄, 福星, or older translated house and planet language.

## Translation Stance

Treat these sources as a Chinese transmission of Western/Islamic/medieval astrology, not as modern psychological astrology and not as pure Ziwei/Bazi. Translate the old terms into the working classical Western method, then judge by the same chain: significator, planet condition, house determination, aspect/reception, timing, and context.

Do not quote or reproduce long classical passages in readings. Use them to sharpen judgment.

The `明译天文书` material is especially basic-method oriented: it gives planet qualities, benefic/malefic categories, sect/day-night associations, oriental/occidental conditions, fixed-star lists, sign categories, aspects, domiciles, and exaltation degrees. Treat it as an old technical glossary. Its own method statement is important: rules are not absolutely fixed; calculation, observation, and repeated testimony are required.

## Basic Quality Logic In Older Chinese Sources

Older translations often describe planets by hot/cold/wet/dry quality:

- Sun: hot and dry; authority, life, visibility.
- Moon: wet and moistening; body, change, growth.
- Saturn: cold and somewhat dry; delay, heaviness, age, land, scarcity.
- Jupiter: warm and moist; protection, honor, law, religion, abundance.
- Mars: extremely hot and dry; cutting, conflict, courage, injury, tools.
- Venus: warm and moist, more moist; union, pleasure, women, art.
- Mercury: variable; takes on the quality of planets it joins or strongly aspects.

Use this quality layer to explain why a planet helps, harms, heats, dries, delays, moistens, or mediates. Do not reduce the judgment to quality alone; house rulership and condition still decide the life area.

The same sources usually classify aspects in simple moral language: trine and sextile help, square and opposition harm, conjunction follows the nature and condition of the planet joined. In actual reading, correct this simple rule by dignity, reception, sect, house topics, and who has superiority.

Fixed-star lists in old Chinese PDFs use ancient coordinates. Recalculate star positions for the native's epoch before judging exact conjunctions.

## House Strength From Tianbu-Style Material

The text strongly prioritizes angular houses and gives a practical strength order:

- First rank: 1st and 10th, then 7th, then 4th.
- Second rank: 11th, 5th, 8th, 2nd.
- Third rank: 9th, 3rd, 6th, 12th.

Use this as a testimony of effectiveness and visibility, not as a moral score. A difficult planet in a strong house can act loudly; a helpful planet in a weak house may help quietly or late.

`明译天文书` gives a related but slightly different practical order: 1st, 10th, 7th, 4th, 11th, 5th, 9th, 3rd, 2nd, 8th, 6th, 12th. Treat house-strength orders as traditional weightings, not an exact arithmetic ladder. If the topic itself is the 2nd, 8th, 6th, or 12th, that house is still central even if its accidental strength is lower.

For cusp judgment, the same material gives a useful rule: a planet within about 5 degrees before the Ascendant can belong to the 1st; beyond that it remains in the 12th. Generalize this cautiously as the standard 5-degree-before-cusp rule, especially for angles.

## Dignity Metaphors

Older Chinese translations explain dignity with social/place metaphors. These are useful in consultation:

- Domicile / 本宫: like a person in their own house; most rooted.
- Exaltation / 旺宫: like a person in a high or noble seat; honored and lifted.
- Triplicity / 三合 dignity: like having allies or favorable kin; supported.
- Term/bound / 界 or 分定度数: like being among relatives or within a permitted jurisdiction; moderate authority.
- Face/decan / 十度: like having a small personal plot or familiar place; minor dignity.
- Fall/detriment/weak places: the planet's environment does not support its nature, so it must borrow help through dispositor, reception, or stronger aspects.

## Life-Stage Planetary Rulers

The Tianbu material gives a general age sequence that resembles planetary ages:

- Moon: birth to about 4 years; body growth, moisture, instability.
- Mercury: about 4 to 14; learning, speech, small knowledge.
- Venus: about 14 to 22; sexuality, pleasure, fertility, social attraction.
- Sun: about 22 to 41; ambition, public aims, honor, larger works.
- Mars: about 41 to 56; struggle, enterprise, anger, labor, action.
- Jupiter: about 56 to 68; counsel, rest, prudence, social respect.
- Saturn: after about 68; decline, coldness, heaviness, old age.

Use these as broad life-period color, not as exact predictive chapters. For exact timing, prefer profections, Firdaria, Solar Arc, progressions, and transits.

## Character And Body Logic

Older Chinese-transmission sources often judge temperament through Moon + Mercury:

- Moon: body, habit, appetite, emotional motion, bodily vitality.
- Mercury: understanding, speech, learning, craft, mental agility.
- Their signs, rulers, aspects, solar condition, speed, and angularity shape the temperament.

For appearance/body, prioritize Ascendant, Ascendant ruler, Moon, Moon sign/ruler, planets on Ascendant, and angular planets. Avoid overconfident physical description; use it more for rectification support than for client-facing certainty.

When Moon/Mercury are disconnected from the Ascendant or heavily dominated by malefics, older texts speak harshly about mental disturbance, folly, or "wind" illness. In modern readings, translate this into stress, cognitive/emotional strain, nervous-system sensitivity, isolation, or need for support; do not diagnose psychiatric conditions from the chart.

## Birth-Time And Historical Prenatal Material

Older sources treat the exact Ascendant degree as decisive and include rectification-like methods using syzygy, Moon position, conception time, and prenatal months. Preserve the useful principle but avoid overusing the old arithmetic:

- Exact birth time controls the Ascendant, lots, house cusps, annual charts, and much of timing.
- If the recorded time is approximate, avoid degree-level lots, fixed stars, and house-cusp claims.
- Ancient conception or prenatal-month rules are historical techniques. Use them only in academic explanation unless the user explicitly asks for old-method reconstruction.
- For practical rectification, prefer dated life events, Solar Arc to angles, profections, progressed Moon, and transits as described in `predictive-timing.md`.
- Longevity, 寿星, and lifespan-number methods belong to historical vitality doctrine. For living people, do not use them to predict death or a fixed lifespan.

## Wealth Logic

Do not use only the 2nd house for wealth. The Tianbu-style method emphasizes Fortune/Fortune ruler and broader support:

- Fortune or wealth significator in angles, above horizon, 11th, or 9th is stronger.
- 2nd house and 2nd lord describe ordinary money and resource handling; major wealth needs stronger support.
- Wealth significator oriental, angular, elevated over other planets, and well-aspected by the native's significators/luminaries is a stronger wealth promise.
- The planet describing wealth shows source: Saturn through land, farming, heavy labor, old goods, water routes, or low-status effort; Jupiter through office, law, patronage, religion, education; Mars through military, conflict, metal, fire, surgery, tools; Venus through women, arts, pleasure, friends; Mercury through learning, writing, trade, calculation, transport.
- Malefics can produce wealth through hard, risky, or morally compromised channels; benefic testimony must be checked before calling it clean prosperity.

For `箭` / lots, remember that the source lists several topic lots, not only Fortune: wealth, siblings, father, and other house topics can have calculated points. Use them only when the formula and exact Ascendant are reliable. In ordinary readings, lots refine the house/lord judgment; they do not replace it.

## Status And Office Logic

Rank is judged by luminaries, angles, 1st/10th, and attending planets:

- Sun and Moon in suitable signs/houses and connected to angles support public quality.
- Luminaries angular or attended by strong planets improve rank.
- 10th and 1st must both be considered: 1st for the native's body/life, 10th for public office and achievement.
- Planets near luminaries or in relation to the 10th describe the source of honor: Mercury through writing/learning, Jupiter/Venus through patrons/friends/benefics, Mars through war/contest/action, Saturn through structure/land/authority or delay.

Use this together with `status-rank.md`.

## Mitigation Logic

The text repeatedly uses vertical strength language: a planet "above" another, in strong houses, or able to oversee can restrain or worsen a matter. Translate this as:

- A benefic or dignified planet with superior position can mitigate damage.
- A malefic in superior position can dominate and harm.
- A harmful significator with benefic testimony may still produce the event, but with rescue, protection, settlement, or survival.
- A benefic significator under malefic domination may still promise good, but the good is contested or harmed.

This matches the classical rule: help and harm depend on strength, aspect, reception, and who has authority.

## Sensitive Topics

Older texts include direct judgments about death, violence, madness, criminality, deformity, and family harm. In modern consultation:

- Do not predict death or accuse a living person.
- Translate severe testimonies into risk, pressure, vulnerability, conflict, or need for support.
- Use harsh classical rules mainly for historical cases, not ordinary client readings.

## Mundane / World-Astrology Material

`明译天文书` also contains mundane methods such as Jupiter-Saturn conjunction cycles, world-period calculations, seasonal ingress-like logic, and judgments about rulers, countries, harvests, warfare, disease, and collective fortune. Keep these separate from natal consultation.

Use mundane material only when the user explicitly asks for world events, countries, cycles, or historical astrology. Do not apply Jupiter-Saturn mundane cycle rules directly to a private natal chart.
