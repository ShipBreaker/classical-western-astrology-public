# Medieval Technique Layer

Use this when the user asks for 中世纪占星, Robert Zoller style reading, 阿慕腾 / Almuten, Arabic Parts / Lots, triplicity rulers, hyleg/alcocoden, or a more rigorous classical predictive framework.

## Local Determination Before Universal Keywords

Medieval practice distinguishes universal/natural significators from local/house significators.

- Universal significator: a planet's general nature, such as Venus for love, Jupiter for wealth, Mercury for merchants/writing, Sun for authority.
- Local significator: a planet's house placement, house rulership, or authority over a specific degree/point.

In concrete delineation, local determination usually gives more specific information. For marriage, Venus matters, but the 7th house, its ruler, planets in the 7th, and their condition must lead the judgment. For money, Jupiter matters, but 2nd/8th/11th/10th testimony must lead.

Practical medieval horoscopy is event- and life-area-oriented. Do not turn it into sun-sign personality writing. Personality comes through the Ascendant, 1st house, 1st lord, Moon, Mercury, angular planets, and their concrete condition; the Sun has special importance but is not a generic "star sign" paragraph.

## Good Or Bad Comes Through The Ruler

A practical medieval rule: the good or bad signified by a house emanates from the ruler of that house.

To judge a house:

1. Inspect the house itself and planets in it.
2. Inspect the house ruler by traditional rulership.
3. Judge the ruler's essential dignity, accidental strength, sect, speed, combustion, retrograde, aspects, and reception.
4. Track where the ruler is placed: that house becomes the arena through which the original house topic acts.
5. Compare natural significators only after this local structure is clear.

This rule is the backbone of 宫主飞星.

## Concrete Language And Topic Translation

Medieval texts often use literal older social categories: slaves, servants, kings, prisons, nobles, temples, ships, and so on. Translate the functional meaning into the client's world while preserving the house logic.

Examples:

- 6th-house "servitude" may appear as employment pressure, heavy labor, subordinates, routine service, illness burden, or being under another person's demands.
- 12th-house "prison" may appear as hospitals, isolation, hidden enemies, institutional confinement, retreat, self-undoing, or working in/with confined institutions.
- 10th-house "king" may appear as government, boss, authority, celebrity, public office, institutional rank, or the person who grants recognition.
- 9th-house "temple/religion" may appear as law, higher education, publication, philosophy, teaching, spiritual practice, foreign systems, or doctrine.

Use elemental/sign qualities as fine-tuning devices for the topic at hand, not as mythological stories. Water signs can support fertility or emotionality in one question, rain/water in mundane work, or fluids in medical symbolism; the topic decides which layer is relevant.

## Almuten

Almuten is the planet with the strongest dignity claim over a degree or point after counting dignity levels such as domicile, exaltation, triplicity, term/bound, and face/decan.

Use Almuten when:

- the user asks for a stricter medieval reading
- several planets compete to signify a house/topic
- judging rank, wealth, profession, vitality, or special lots
- the source chart gives degree-level dignity tables

Do not invent Almuten from memory. Use a reliable dignity table or chart software. If only sign-level data is available, state that Almuten cannot be confirmed.

A common medieval scoring model is domicile 5, exaltation 4, triplicity 3, term/bound 2, face/decan 1. Use this only when the dignity table and degree are known; otherwise keep the reading qualitative.

## Triplicity Rulers

Triplicity rulers describe support over time, especially for life chapters, houses, and durability of a topic.

Use them as a secondary layer:

- First triplicity ruler: early or primary support.
- Second triplicity ruler: middle or alternate support.
- Participating ruler: background or cooperating support.

Exact triplicity tables differ by tradition. Use the table supplied by the source/chart software and state the tradition if it matters.

## Arabic Parts / Lots

Lots convert relationships between planets and angles into topical points. The most important:

- Lot of Fortune: body, material circumstance, what happens to the native, fortune, physical life.
- Lot of Spirit: intention, action, career, mind, chosen direction, soul/intellect.
- Lot of Eros: love, desire, relationship themes.

Use lot formulas that adjust for sect when required by the tradition. Do not calculate lots casually if the chart's day/night condition or exact Ascendant is uncertain.

Read a lot by:

1. Sign and house of the lot.
2. Ruler of the lot.
3. Planets aspecting or occupying the lot's sign/house.
4. Condition of the lot ruler.
5. Timing systems that release from or activate the lot.

Do not assume a lot is active just because it exists. For example, the Lot of Fortune can be central for wealth only when its ruler or dignity lords can actually witness, support, or determine it. If Fortune and its rulers are disconnected or afflicted, other money significators may dominate. Fortune can also be relevant to body, health, and material circumstances rather than cash alone.

## Hyleg And Alcocoden

Hyleg and Alcocoden belong to traditional vitality/longevity analysis. Treat this as specialist historical technique, not routine client prediction.

For living people, do not use these to predict death. If studying historical cases, explain the method academically and keep uncertainty explicit.

## Predictive Layers

Medieval timing is layered:

- Profections: annual house and Lord of the Year.
- Firdaria / 法达: broad planetary chapters.
- Triplicity periods: long-range support for topics.
- Primary directions / directions through terms: precise but calculation-heavy.
- Solar returns: annual chart as a layer over natal promise.
- Transits: triggers and visible circumstances.
- Zodiacal Releasing: Hellenistic time-lord release from lots.

Each layer activates natal promise. A timing method does not create an unsupported event.

## Practical Reading Sequence

When using this layer in a reading:

1. State the natal promise through houses and rulers.
2. Add essential/accidental dignity and reception.
3. Add Almuten or triplicity rulers only if data supports them.
4. Add lots when they clarify the topic.
5. Add timing layers in order from broad chapter to narrow trigger.
6. Require convergence before making a confident judgment.

## Caution

Medieval sources can sound absolute. Translate them into modern consultation language. For example, "bad fortune through the 8th" may become debt pressure, anxiety, business-risk exposure, partner-resource problems, or crisis-management themes depending on the full chart.
