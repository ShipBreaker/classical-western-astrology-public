# Predictive Timing Logic

## First Rule: Natal Promise Before Timing

Do not predict an event type that the natal chart does not support. Timing techniques activate, unfold, or describe the timing of natal potential; they do not create a completely foreign life promise.

Workflow:

1. Define the topic and natal promise.
2. Identify the natal significators and their condition.
3. Choose timing techniques appropriate to the question.
4. Look for two or three independent testimonies converging on the same topic and time window.
5. Use transits or progressed Moon for activation and shorter timing.

Keep age, life stage, and social form in the forecast. A symbol that can mean marriage, leadership, inheritance, illness, or travel must be scaled to the person's age and context before it becomes a prediction.

## Integrated Timing Stack

The ZOE-style timing notes summarize the practical workflow well:

1. Make the natal structure clear first.
2. Stack repeated testimonies of the same kind; do not chase every isolated symbol.
3. Distinguish the concrete topic by changing the reference point when needed, such as using derived houses for partner, child, parent, company, or client questions.
4. Use timing tools as activators. A tool is useful when it narrows the natal promise, not when it creates a new story.
5. Know each tool's scale: Firdaria/ZR/profection for chapter and topic, Solar Arc/primary-like direction for year-level peaks, secondary progressions for unfolding environment, transits/eclipses/progressed Moon for activation.

For topic questions, phrase the forecast as "the chart shows this field is activated, and the most likely concrete forms are..." rather than pretending one technique alone names the outcome.

## Age Of Man / Planetary Life Periods

Use the Ptolemaic "Age of Man" scheme as a broad life-stage background, not as exact event timing:

- Moon: birth to about age 4, bodily growth, dependence, nourishment, rapid change.
- Mercury: about 4-14, learning, speech, curiosity, early skills.
- Venus: about 14-22, attraction, sexuality, social pleasure, aesthetic awakening.
- Sun: about 22-41, adulthood, ambition, honor, status, self-direction.
- Mars: about 41-56, labor, contest, anxiety, striving, physical effort.
- Jupiter: about 56-68, counsel, reputation, judgment, legacy, release from heavy labor.
- Saturn: about 68 onward, age, withdrawal, limitation, endurance, final consolidation.

When an event signature appears, ask whether it fits the life period. The same 7th-house activation may show first desire in the Venus age, legal marriage in the Sun age, litigation or public opponents in the Mars age, or spouse health/support issues in later ages.

## Annual Profections

Use annual profections to identify the year's activated house and Lord of the Year.

Judge:

- Activated house topic.
- Lord of the Year natal condition.
- Lord of the Year current transit condition.
- Transits to the profected house, its lord, Ascendant, MC, Sun, Moon.

Annual profection is a framing tool. It becomes more persuasive when solar return, transit, or other timing techniques repeat the same houses/planets.

Annual profection moves one house or sign per birthday year. Age 0 activates the 1st house, age 1 the 2nd, and so on, returning to the 1st every 12 years. Use actual age from birthday to birthday, not lunar nominal age.

For a fuller 宫限 / 小限 reading, inspect:

- Activated house and its topics.
- Lord of the Year by traditional rulership.
- Natal condition of the Lord of the Year.
- Solar return placement and condition of the Lord of the Year.
- Natal and transiting planets in the activated house.
- Transits to the activated house, Lord of the Year, Ascendant, MC, Sun, Moon, and topic rulers.

Monthly profection begins from the annual profected house at the birthday and advances one house each month. Daily profection can be approximated as one house every 2.5 days when a short window is needed, but use transits and progressed Moon for tighter practical timing.

Profection can also be applied to specific points: Ascendant for body/life direction, Sun for honor/status, Moon for body and soul rhythms, MC for career, Fortune or 2nd cusp for money. Treat these as topic-specific activation tools and do not demand modern exact-aspect precision from them.

Practical refinements:

- Whole-sign or equal-style profection is usually the cleanest first pass. Quadrant houses can be checked when the source chart uses them, but do not turn annual profection into a house-system argument.
- The profected house can be treated as a temporary Ascendant or 太极点 for derived-house work, but this quickly becomes complex. Use it only when the question needs conversion, such as partner, child, company, or parent topics.
- Daily profection at about 2.5 days per house exists, but it is usually too fine for ordinary consultation. Prefer transits or progressed Moon for short windows.
- Profection repeats every 12 years. The concrete event must be scaled by age, social context, natal promise, and other timing testimony. A 7th-house year can be dating, marriage, clients, lawsuits, public opponents, partner illness, or separation depending on the chart.
- Do not predict death or a singular extreme event from profection alone, because the same house returns every 12 years.
- Experimental "move all planets one sign per year" profection can sometimes match events within one or two months, but treat it as a research supplement rather than the core method.

For finance by profection, inspect the profected 2nd house, its lord, benefics/malefics in that house, and afflictions to the profected 2nd lord. The afflicting planet's nature and house often describe the source of expense or loss. Malefics can still bring money, but usually through labor, conflict, risk, debt, or pressure.

## Firdaria / 法达

Use Firdaria for broad life chapters ruled by planets. Judge the major period lord and subperiod lord by:

- Natal dignity and house placement.
- Houses ruled by the lord.
- Aspects/reception with natal topic significators.
- Whether the planet is of sect and capable of delivering constructive results.

Firdaria is better for chapter tone and recurring themes than exact dates.

If the Sun is very near the horizon, classify day/night with care. A Sun very near the Ascendant at dawn can be treated as day in this teaching stream; a Sun near the Descendant at sunset may need event validation. Name the ambiguity if it changes the sequence.

Day-birth major sequence:

- Sun 10 years.
- Venus 8 years.
- Mercury 13 years.
- Moon 9 years.
- Saturn 11 years.
- Jupiter 12 years.
- Mars 7 years.
- North Node 3 years.
- South Node 2 years.

Night-birth major sequence in the common node-free Bonatti/Zoller-style form:

- Moon 9 years.
- Saturn 11 years.
- Jupiter 12 years.
- Mars 7 years.
- Sun 10 years.
- Venus 8 years.
- Mercury 13 years.

If using a node-including night sequence, place the nodes after Mercury: North Node 3 years, then South Node 2 years, extending the sequence to 75 years. The middle-node placement after Mars is a less common variant; use it only when the source chart or software explicitly follows that system, and name the variant.

Subperiods divide each planetary major period into seven subperiods, beginning with the major period lord and then following the Chaldean order used by the Firdaria sequence. Node periods are usually not subdivided in this teaching stream.

Firdaria boundary dates are not knife-cut exact. Allow roughly a month around a subperiod change before insisting the new lord has fully taken over.

Reading rule: first define what the period lord promises in the natal chart by nature, house occupied, houses ruled, dignity, aspects, reception, and sect. Then blend the subperiod lord. A major/subperiod pair is most eventful when both link the same houses or significators. For example, marriage needs repeated 1st/7th testimony and age-appropriate context; a childhood 1st/7th activation should not be read as legal marriage.

When the major lord and subperiod lord have natal relation by aspect, reception, mutual reception, antiscia, contra-antiscia, or shared house topics, prioritize the combined structure. If they have no meaningful natal connection, read the subperiod lord's own natal condition more independently.

Firdaria can be treated as a structure activator:

- A major period can begin the preparation phase before visible success appears.
- The decisive event often arrives in the subperiod that connects the major lord to the 1st, 10th, money houses, or the exact topic structure.
- Repeated peaks can occur whenever the same helpful subperiod lord returns under different major lords, especially when Solar Arc or profections also support it.
- If a period activates wealth houses but not the 10th or career platform, it may bring money movement, capital preparation, or risk rather than public rise.
- If a period activates 1R/10R but not 2nd/8th/11th, it may bring office, title, visibility, or responsibility rather than cash.
- A benefic period is not automatically productive if that benefic rules irrelevant houses or encourages comfort, delay, or complacency. A malefic period is not automatically bad if the malefic rules the career/money structure and has reception or benefic rescue, although it may still cost health, rest, or peace.
- A debilitated planet can deliver results if rescued by strong benefics or useful reception; the debility often describes leakage, instability, expense, or inability to keep what is gained.
- Periods often deliver both sides of the planet: a planet tied to 10th and 8th can bring career/resource opportunity and crisis/health pressure in the same chapter.

Do not read a good Firdaria period as guaranteed success. It must activate a real natal structure and fit the social environment.

## Zodiacal Releasing

Use Zodiacal Releasing when the chart data and calculation are available. Do not hand-compute it casually.

General use:

- Release from Spirit for career, action, purpose, and public life.
- Release from Fortune for bodily/material circumstances and events that happen to the native.
- Release from Eros can be used for love, relationship, desire, and sometimes friendship questions when that lot is calculated reliably.
- Peak periods, angular relationships, loosening of the bond, and lord condition describe intensity and turning points.
- Judge the activated sign itself, the lord of that sign, and the angularity of the activated sign relative to Fortune.
- Benefic and malefic aspects to the activated sign modify ease, support, pressure, and difficulty.

Always bring the releasing period back to the natal condition of its sign ruler.

Additional ZR rules:

- ZR divides life into activated signs and time-lord chapters. A sign can be activated even when empty; planets configured to it by conjunction, square, or opposition still color the period.
- The period lengths by sign follow domicile lord minor years: Cancer 25, Leo 19, Virgo/Gemini 20, Libra/Taurus 8, Scorpio/Aries 15, Sagittarius/Pisces 12, Capricorn 27, Aquarius 30.
- Capricorn 27 / Aquarius 30 follows the Valens-style route used here. Some sources or software assign both Saturn signs 30; if the supplied calculation uses that convention, follow it and name the difference.
- Levels are hierarchical: Level 1 years, Level 2 months, Level 3 weeks, Level 4 days; lower levels are subdivisions of the higher period and should not override the main chapter.
- When releasing from Spirit and Spirit is in the same sign as Fortune, some Valens-derived practice advances Spirit one sign for the release. Name this rule if you use it.
- Angular signs from Fortune are peak periods: 1st/4th/7th/10th from Fortune show highest activity, succedent signs carry or stabilize, cadent signs begin or reduce activity. A peak can be difficult if malefics dominate that sign.
- Sect modifies subjective ease: the benefic of sect tends to make periods feel more supportive; the malefic contrary to sect tends to make periods more challenging.
- Loosing of the Bond marks a major sequence break or transition. Compare the target sign to its earlier activation; the earlier activation often foreshadows the later one.
- To forecast with ZR, study the person's past periods first. Repeated sign activations often repeat themes, although the concrete manifestation changes with age, environment, and other timing layers.

## Solar And Lunar Returns

Solar return is the chart for the exact annual return of the Sun to its natal position. Lunar return is the chart for the Moon's return to its natal position.

Use:

- Solar return for the year tone, annual circumstances, and topic support.
- Lunar return for shorter emotional/body/monthly emphasis when the larger timing already exists.
- The current residence or the place where the person is actually living at the return as the practical default in this teaching stream.

Simple method: read the return chart as a temporary independent chart, then compare it to the natal chart. This is useful but incomplete.

Return charts can produce too many testimonies. Do not let one return chart override natal promise, Firdaria/ZR/profection, Solar Arc, or strong progressions/transits. Use returns as support and topic clarification, not as the sole forecast.

## Solar Arc

Solar Arc is useful for major life peaks and visible events.

Principles:

- Approximate one degree equals one year for quick age estimation.
- Calculate the true solar arc from progressed Sun minus natal Sun when precision matters.
- When estimating by simple degrees, allow seasonal drift: births near winter can perfect slightly earlier than a flat one-degree-per-year estimate, and births near summer can perfect slightly later. Use true Solar Arc software for serious timing.
- Move planets and angles by that arc.
- Give priority to Solar Arc contacts to Ascendant, MC, Sun, Moon, Ascendant ruler, MC ruler, and topic rulers.
- Use Solar Arc for approximate year, then confirm with progressed Moon and transits for timing.
- In the Noel Tyl style material, prioritize hard contacts: conjunction, square, opposition. Semi-square can be noted only when already supported.
- The effective window is roughly one year around exactness; the first half of the year before exactness is often stronger than the fading half after exactness.
- Several Solar Arc contacts perfecting in the same short period are more important than a single isolated contact.
- Direction matters: SA Saturn to natal Mercury is not identical to SA Mercury to natal Saturn. Read the directed planet as the moving agent and the natal planet as the field being contacted.
- Childhood Solar Arc contacts, especially to angles, often describe parents, home, or family circumstances rather than the child's own adult-style event.

Interpretation examples:

- Solar Arc Sun to MC: visibility, recognition, public direction, authority themes.
- Solar Arc MC to Jupiter/Venus: opportunity, honor, support, relationship or benefit depending on natal house links.
- Solar Arc malefic to Asc/MC/luminary: pressure, responsibility, conflict, health or career stress depending on natal promise.

Solar Arc symbolism must fit age and reality. Venus to MC may show love, child, art, public favor, or social benefit; choose by natal houses and life context.

Angle contacts are paired. Solar Arc contacts to Ascendant also implicate Descendant; contacts to MC also implicate IC. A hit to the Ascendant can manifest through the spouse, partner, client, or open opponent when the 7th-house testimony is stronger. A hit to MC can manifest through family, parents, residence, or land when the IC/4th testimony is stronger.

Do not universalize a single Solar Arc formula. For example, Solar Arc MC to Saturn or Saturn to MC can mark parent/family loss, heavy responsibility, career burden, or authority pressure. Choose by natal Saturn's house rulership, condition, and whether parent/family houses repeat the testimony.

Modern Solar Arc practice often emphasizes outer planets and midpoint pictures. In this classical skill, use them as optional timing modifiers, not as replacement for natal promise, house rulership, sect, and dignity. If the user wants a Tyl-style reading, you may describe Uranus/Neptune/Pluto contacts more directly, but still route the event through natal houses and life context.

For career or rank peaks, a practical sequence is: Firdaria or profection identifies the chapter, Solar Arc gives the visible year window, progressed Moon and transits trigger the month, and biography confirms whether the manifestation was office/title, public recognition, education milestone, relocation, family event, or resource change.

Solar Arc is especially useful for scanning many years quickly. Compared with casting a separate solar return for every year, Solar Arc lets you see which years carry hard contacts to angles, luminaries, and topic rulers.

Solar Arc rectification rule: MC moves about 1 degree for every 4 minutes of clock time, and Solar Arc treats about 1 degree as about 1 year. If a Solar Arc angle event appears two years too early or too late, the candidate birth time may be off by about eight minutes. Adjust in the direction that makes the known event perfect in the correct year, then validate with three to five dated events.

For rectification, prioritize:

- Solar Arc MC/Asc to natal planets.
- Solar Arc planets to natal MC/Asc.
- Events that visibly change status, family, residence, career, marriage, divorce, child, surgery, parent events, awards, public scandal, or major relocation.
- Natal house/ruler logic first. If the candidate Ascendant or MC cannot explain the life pattern, do not force Solar Arc to rescue it.

If birth time is known only within a two-hour branch, Solar Arc can often narrow it. If only the birth date is known, Solar Arc alone is usually not enough.

## Secondary Progressions / 次限

Core rule: one day after birth equals one year of life.

Use:

- Read the progressed chart both as its own symbolic chart and as an overlay to the natal chart; the overlay to natal planets, Ascendant, MC, and topic rulers is usually cleaner for concrete events.
- Progressed Sun for long identity and life-direction shifts.
- Progressed Moon as the main trigger and mood/environment indicator.
- Progressed Mercury/Venus/Mars for multi-year mental, relational, financial, and action themes.
- Progressed Jupiter/Saturn mostly for long-term trend, station, or exact contact; slow movement means repeated natal patterns may dominate. Pay attention when progressed Jupiter/Saturn tighten to a natal point or station/direct by progression.
- Progressed angles are birth-time sensitive but high priority. Contacts involving progressed Ascendant/MC or progressed planets to natal Ascendant/MC often describe large directional changes.

Orb discipline:

- Keep most progressed aspects near 1 degree.
- Progressed Sun/Moon may be stretched slightly when supported; progressed Moon can be allowed roughly up to 3 degrees, but exactness matters for event peaks. A progressed Moon contact can feel like a three-month window, with the middle month strongest.
- Avoid wide orbs. Slow progressions become vague if the orb is too loose.

Compare progressed planets to natal planets, Ascendant, MC, and topic rulers. Progressions show the internal and unfolding environment; transits often describe external activation.

The progressed Moon is especially noticeable when the natal chart strongly emphasizes the Moon or Cancer, when the Moon is dignified, angular, or tied to the Ascendant, and when the progressed Moon hits an already active natal/timing structure. Do not overread every progressed Moon aspect by itself.

For relationship timing, progressed Venus or Mercury in the 7th is not enough by itself. Look for contacts to the Sun, Mars, Ascendant ruler, 7th ruler, Venus, Moon, Descendant, or 5th/7th significators. The symbolism may show relationship connection, dating, desire, or partnership emphasis; legal marriage registration is a social form and needs additional testimony.

Tertiary and minor progressions can be used for shorter windows if the chart software provides them, but treat them as refiners after the main testimony is already present through natal promise, annual/profection/Firdaria/ZR/Solar Arc, secondary progressions, or transits.

## Tertiary Progressions / 三限

Tertiary progressions use a shorter scale than secondary progressions. In the teaching stream, the working idea is one day after birth equals about one lunar month or one actual month of life, depending on the software convention.

Use tertiary progressions for short-term observation and event refinement after larger testimony exists.

Practical notes:

- They are better for month-level self-study, especially if the native keeps a diary of events and moods.
- Read them with the same principles as secondary progressions: contacts to natal planets, angles, Ascendant ruler, MC ruler, and topic rulers matter most.
- A secondary progression that lasts two to three years can show as a two to three month tertiary theme.
- A secondary Mars theme of four to five years can compress into a four to five month tertiary theme.
- Tertiary Moon can move too quickly and often becomes a one or two week signal. Use it lightly unless it hits an already active natal/timing structure.
- Tertiary angles and tertiary contacts to 1st/6th/8th/12th significators can be useful for health-warning windows, but never diagnose. Say "health pressure / need to check the body" rather than naming a disease.

In practice, tertiary progressions are refiners. Do not build a major forecast only from a tertiary contact.

## Transits

Use transits as triggers and circumstances, not as isolated destiny.

Prioritize:

- Saturn, Jupiter, Mars to angles, luminaries, Ascendant ruler, MC ruler, and topic rulers.
- Outer planets only as background/modern modifiers unless the user requests a modern blend.
- Fast planets for date refinement when slower testimony already exists.

Interpret transits through natal rulership. A transit to Venus means different things when Venus rules the 2nd and 9th than when Venus rules the 6th and 11th.

Transit discipline:

- Slow planets provide the larger weather; faster planets time the day/week only when the larger testimony exists.
- A planet crossing a house is weaker than a tight contact to a natal planet, angle, or topic ruler, unless the house contains important natal planets.
- Retrograde cycles matter. A direct hit, retrograde hit, and final direct hit can show a three-part story: first contact, review/entanglement, resolution or final manifestation.
- Eclipses can amplify a topic when they fall tightly on angles, luminaries, Ascendant ruler, MC ruler, or the relevant house ruler/cusp. Treat them as triggers or spotlight events, not as standalone fate.
- Topic triggers: Sun/Moon/Asc for general change; IC/4th/Moon for home and relocation; Venus/Desc/7th for marriage and partnership; MC/10th for career; 2nd/8th for money; 6th/Asc/Moon for health; Mercury/Jupiter/3rd/9th for education, writing, travel, and exams.
- Repeating age cycles such as Jupiter return, Saturn return, progressed Moon return, and the early-40s Saturn/Uranus/Neptune complex are background chapters. They become personal events only through natal and topical links.

## Rectification / 生时校正

Use rectification only when enough dated events exist.

Procedure:

1. Collect high-confidence life events with dates: relocation, parent events, school/career transitions, marriage/divorce, birth of children, illness/surgery, accidents, awards, deaths in family, legal events.
2. Identify likely houses and significators for each event.
3. Test angle-sensitive techniques: Solar Arc to angles, profected Ascendant/house, transits to angles, progressed Moon over angles.
4. Adjust birth time conservatively and record why a candidate time fits or fails.

Do not force rectification from one event. Prefer a small set of candidate times with evidence.

## Forecast Output

For prediction, answer in this order:

1. Natal promise: what can happen in this area.
2. Current activation: which technique activates which planet/house.
3. Time window: year/month range, with exactness only if testimony supports it.
4. Likely manifestation: several plausible concrete forms, ranked by chart context.
5. Advice: what to do with the timing.
6. Boundary: missing data or uncertain birth time.
