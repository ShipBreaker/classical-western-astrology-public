# Natal Reading Logic

## First Principle

Do not begin with "Sun in sign" personality paragraphs. Begin with the chart's governing structure: Ascendant, Ascendant ruler, MC/10th, luminaries, sect, angular planets, and the network of house rulers.

The reading sequence is:

1. Determine the chart frame.
2. Judge planetary capacity.
3. Judge house topics.
4. Connect topics through rulership, placement, aspects, and reception.
5. Synthesize repeated testimony into a concrete life pattern.

## Chart Frame

Before interpretation, record:

- Zodiac and house system used.
- Birth time reliability and whether angles can be trusted.
- Day or night chart.
- Ascendant sign, Ascendant ruler, MC/10th sign and ruler.
- Angular planets and planets close to Asc/MC/Desc/IC.
- Any obvious stellium, luminary emphasis, or strong benefic/malefic.

If the birth time is uncertain, avoid angle-dependent claims and emphasize sign, planet, and non-time-sensitive patterns.

## Planetary Capacity

For each major significator, judge three layers:

- Nature: what the planet naturally means.
- Celestial state: dignity, debility, sect, speed, combustion, retrograde, lunar phase, fixed stars/nodes if relevant.
- Terrestrial state: house placement, angularity, house rulership, relationship to angles.

A planet that is naturally benefic but damaged may bring benefit with waste, dependency, excess, or delay. A planet that is naturally malefic but dignified and well-placed may bring discipline, technical skill, endurance, command, surgery, engineering, law enforcement, or other constructive hard functions.

## Topic Judgment Formula

For any topic, identify:

1. Topic house.
2. House lord by traditional rulership.
3. Planets in that house.
4. Natural significators.
5. Almuten or special lots if the technique and data support them.
6. Aspects to the house lord and occupants.
7. Reception/mutual reception/dispositor chain.
8. Condition of the relevant houses derived from the topic.

Then ask:

- Can the significator act? Judge dignity and accidental strength.
- Where does it act? Judge house placement and rulership.
- Who helps or harms it? Judge aspects, reception, benefics/malefics.
- Is the testimony repeated? Major conclusions need more than one indication.

## Subject / 用神 Discipline

Some Chinese classical-astrology teaching phrases the first move as finding the "主语" or "用神". In practice, this means translating the user's question into astrological grammar before judging good or bad.

For any concrete question, ask:

- Who or what is the subject? Use the topic house cusp, house lord, planets in the house, and natural significator.
- What action or condition is being judged? Use the relevant aspect, reception, dignity, affliction, help, or timing planet.
- Who is affected? Use the native's significators, the other person's significators, or derived houses.

Example logic: for "someone harms my marriage", do not jump to scandal. First map "someone" by context such as 3rd siblings/neighbors, 5th lover or partner's friends by derivation, 11th friends/groups; map "harm" through a malefic, hard aspect, harmful reception, or destructive dispositor chain; map "marriage" through the 7th, 7th lord, planets in 7th, Venus, and traditional spouse significators. Only then judge whether the chart shows conflict, temptation, outside pressure, ordinary disagreement, or nothing strong enough to state.

One house contains several possible manifestations. The 5th may be children, romance, pleasure, art, performance, speculation, or entertainment. A difficult 5th signature might show investment loss, romantic frustration, child-related pressure, or creative blockage. Choose the concrete form by client context, repeated house links, age, and timing; otherwise describe the field rather than pretending the chart names a single event.

## Event-Level Versus Character-Level

Natural planet meanings alone describe style and temperament. Event-level statements require house testimony.

Example logic:

- Mercury afflicted without 3rd/9th/1st/10th involvement may show sharp, restless, or pressured thinking.
- Mercury afflicted as 3rd lord, in the 3rd, ruling the 9th, or tied to travel/study houses can show learning, examination, communication, traffic, or sibling events.
- Jupiter well-aspected does not automatically mean wealth. It becomes financial testimony when tied to 2nd, 8th, 10th, 11th, Fortune, or their rulers.

This is the key professional distinction: star nature gives texture; house nature gives life area.

## Context And Proportion

Treat the chart as a symbolic map inside a real world, not as a sealed machine. Before making a concrete judgment, adjust for:

- Age: a 7th-house or Venus activation in childhood may describe parents, family social life, or early bonding, not marriage.
- Country, era, class, and custom: the same marriage, career, or education testimony manifests through the society available to the native.
- Family environment and upbringing: natal promise may be supported, redirected, delayed, or suppressed by circumstances.
- Collective events: war, epidemic, disaster, migration, and policy can override individual chart differences; do not expect every participant to show the same personal signature.
- User agency and appetite for risk: some chart potentials remain potentials because the native does not choose the corresponding path.

This protects the reading from false precision. The chart shows a structured field of tendencies and timing, while biography shows which path inside that field became real.

## Main Life Pattern

For a full natal reading, prioritize:

1. 1st house and Ascendant ruler: body, temperament, life path, what the native carries.
2. 10th house/MC and ruler: visible action, career, rank, vocation, public direction.
3. Sun and Moon: vitality, identity, body rhythm, parents, visibility, inner/outer life.
4. The sect benefic and out-of-sect malefic: where support comes easily and where life tests are sharper.
5. Strong angular or dignified planets: planets that dominate the life story.
6. Repeated house links: for example 1/10/2/11 links often matter for career and income; 1/7/5 links for relationship and romance; 1/6/8/12 links for health and crisis.

The 1st and 10th are especially important for the overall "格局": if they are strong and well-supported, weak 2nd-house testimony may show financial instability rather than poverty; if they are weak, a good 2nd house may show basic resources without high status.

## Synthesis Rules

- Strong testimony: repeated through house lord, occupant, natural significator, angle, dignity, and timing.
- Mixed testimony: one side gives opportunity while another gives cost, delay, conflict, or instability.
- Weak testimony: isolated keyword, wide aspect, no house relevance, or unsupported outer-planet symbolism.
- Contradictory testimony: state both sides and judge which planet has more power by dignity, angularity, sect, and exactness.

When explaining, show the strongest two or three testimonies. Do not drown the user in every minor factor.

## Consultation Conduct

Ask for the user's concrete question when the scope is vague. A good reading answers a question, not every possible symbol in the chart.

Use "倾向 / 容易 / 更像 / 需要验证 / 不能仅凭此断" for uncertain or sensitive conclusions. When a chart suggests difficulty, give practical framing: where pressure appears, what kind of choices reduce harm, and what timing or context matters.
