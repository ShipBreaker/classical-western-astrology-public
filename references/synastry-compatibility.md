# Synastry And Relationship Compatibility

Use this file for 合盘, 比较盘, synastry, composite chart, Davison chart, 马克思盘, relationship compatibility, marriage matching, breakup analysis, and partner dynamics.

## Core Stance

Synastry is a modern technique. In this skill, keep it subordinate to the natal charts:

1. Read person A's natal relationship promise.
2. Read person B's natal relationship promise.
3. Then compare the two charts.
4. Use timing only after the natal and synastry structure is clear.

Do not treat a synastry aspect list as fate. A difficult contact can become conflict, responsibility, growth, distance, or separation depending on the natal charts, life stage, maturity, and timing.

## Relationship Type First

Before judging, name the relationship form:

- Dating, attraction, short romance: 5th house, Venus, Mars, Moon, sexual/pleasure indicators.
- Long-term cohabitation and daily life: Moon, 4th, 7th, Saturn, Ascendant compatibility.
- Legal marriage or stable contract: 7th, 1st/7th rulers, Saturn, MC/social status, family houses.
- Business partnership: 7th, 2nd/8th, 10th/11th, Mercury, Jupiter, Saturn.
- Parent-child or family: 4th/10th, Moon, Sun/Saturn/Venus, derived houses.
- Teacher-student, patron, alliance: 9th/10th/11th, Jupiter, Mercury, Saturn.

The same contact changes meaning by relationship type. Mars to Venus can be sexual attraction in dating, argument and irritation in marriage, or productive drive in business if other testimony supports it.

## Read Each Natal Chart First

For each person, inspect:

- 1st and Ascendant ruler: temperament, self-protection, how the person enters relationship.
- 5th and 5th ruler: romance, pleasure, dating, children, sexual play, creative joy.
- 7th and 7th ruler: spouse, contract, partner type, open conflict, legal tie.
- Venus and Mars: attraction, desire, style of pursuit and response.
- Moon: emotional need, daily bonding, home rhythm, cohabitation tolerance.
- Sun: identity, will, male/father/authority images when relevant.
- Mercury: communication style and the ability to talk through conflict.
- Saturn: commitment, burden, fear, delay, duty, age gap, endurance, dryness.

Ask whether the person is seeking love, companionship, children, status, money, safety, novelty, escape, or a legally stable life. The natal chart often explains why the person chooses a relationship that looks confusing from synastry alone.

## Inter-Chart Aspect Priority

Prioritize tight inter-chart aspects involving:

- Moon: long-term emotional life. Moon hard contacts are often felt strongly because they affect need, habit, safety, and daily living.
- Sun: identity, pride, direction, male image, vitality. Sun hard contacts can show ego conflict or incompatible life direction.
- Venus/Mars: attraction, sex, pleasure, flirtation, irritation, pursuit.
- Mercury: whether the two can talk, understand, negotiate, and share interests.
- Saturn: commitment and endurance, but also coldness, fear, pressure, distance, obligation.
- 1st/7th rulers and angles: strong relationship event or "cannot ignore each other" testimony.
- Nodes, Vertex, asteroids such as Juno or Lilith: optional modern modifiers only after the main planets and houses are clear.

Use classical judgment even for modern synastry: dignity, reception, house rulership, and the natal condition of each planet decide whether the contact is helpful, harmful, heavy, or merely stimulating.

## House Overlays

House overlays are secondary to inter-chart aspects, but still useful.

Read an overlay by asking:

- Which house in A is activated by B's planet?
- Is that house already important in A's natal chart?
- Does B's planet have dignity, affliction, or strong natal rulership?
- Does A have planets in that house or a sensitive ruler that makes the overlay easier to feel?

If A's 5th or 7th house is empty and not emphasized, B's planet falling there may be less vivid than expected. If A's 5th/7th ruler is already central, the overlay can become much more noticeable.

## Stage Changes

Relationships change form:

- Friend to lover changes expectations around exclusivity, desire, jealousy, and emotional access.
- Lover to spouse adds family, law, money, housework, parents, children, and social status.
- Spouse to co-parent adds 5th-house and family obligations.

Do not judge a relationship only by the early attraction chart. Ask which stage the user is asking about.

## External Factor Or Internal Factor

Synastry mainly shows how two people interact. It does not always show whether the relationship ends because of internal mismatch or external pressure.

For external causes, return to the natal charts and timing:

- Illness, debt, bankruptcy, relocation, parent/family pressure, career collapse, children, law, scandal, or death must be supported by the relevant natal houses and timing.
- If the relationship itself looks workable but one person's natal timing is strongly pressured, the problem may come from life circumstances rather than lack of affection.
- If the same relationship conflict repeats in a person's natal chart across partners, treat it as their natal relationship pattern.

## Marriage Timing

A 7th-house activation is a relationship or partnership event; it is not automatically legal marriage.

For marriage, require:

- Age and cultural context where marriage is realistic.
- Natal promise for partnership/marriage.
- 1st/7th testimony, preferably with 5th, Venus/Moon/Sun/Mars, MC/social status, or family houses.
- Timing convergence from profection, Firdaria/ZR, Solar Arc, progressions, transits, or returns.

Legal registration is a social form. The chart may show a relationship window, cohabitation, engagement, pregnancy, public status change, or breakup instead.

## Composite, Davison, And Marks Charts

Use these as optional modern supplements:

- Synastry / 比较盘: two natal charts overlaid. Best for how two people stimulate each other.
- Composite / 组合盘: mathematical midpoint chart of the relationship. Useful for the "relationship entity"; can be progressed by some modern astrologers, but treat this as experimental.
- Davison / 时空盘: chart for the midpoint in time and space. Useful as another relationship entity chart when birth data is reliable.
- Marks / 马克思盘 or 马盘: a niche Chinese-language practice. Do not pretend mastery if the user requests detailed Marks-chart rules; say this skill treats it as supplementary unless a clear method is supplied.

For timing in ordinary synastry, usually time each person's natal chart separately. A synastry chart itself does not have a clean independent progression unless using composite/Davison methods.

## Difficult Relationship Contacts

Translate difficult contacts into practical language:

- Saturn to Moon: responsibility, emotional coldness, fear, dependence, age/maturity gap, endurance through duty.
- Saturn to Venus/Mars: delay, frustration, dryness, obligation, commitment mixed with dissatisfaction.
- Mars to Moon/Venus: heat, desire, irritation, arguments, physical attraction, emotional volatility.
- Pluto/Neptune/Uranus contacts: modern modifiers for compulsion, idealization, confusion, rupture, novelty, freedom, or sudden change. Route them through natal house rulership and timing before making event claims.
- Nodes to planets: a strong feeling of connection, familiarity, obligation, or repeated entanglement. It can be good, bad, or neutral depending on the planet contacted and its natal condition.

Do not tell a living client "you must break up" from one contact. Say where the pressure lies, what would have to mature, and whether the broader chart supports staying, restructuring, or leaving.

## Consultation Output

For relationship readings, answer in this order:

1. Each person's natal relationship pattern.
2. Strongest synastry contacts.
3. Attraction and bonding factors.
4. Friction and long-term stress factors.
5. Whether the issue is internal to the relationship or likely external.
6. Timing window if asked.
7. Practical guidance and boundaries.
