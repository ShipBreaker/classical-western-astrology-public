# Topic Modules

Use these modules after the general chart frame is known.

## Career And Status

Primary houses: 1st, 10th/MC, 6th, 2nd, 11th.

Judge:

- 10th lord, MC, planets in/near 10th, and planets aspecting MC.
- Ascendant ruler and its link to the 10th.
- 6th for daily work, skills, labor environment, subordinates.
- 2nd for income and resources; 11th for patrons, networks, gains from career.
- Sun for authority/visibility, Saturn for structure/administration, Mars for action/technical conflict, Mercury for trade/writing/calculation, Venus for art/beauty/social mediation, Jupiter for law/education/finance/protection.
- For profession/craft specifically, compare the 10th, 10th lord, planets in the 10th, the strongest of Mercury/Venus/Mars, and any reliable professional lot. Choose the planet that has both strength and connection to the career house or Ascendant.

Career content comes from house links first, then planet nature and sign style. For example, 1/10 tied to 3/9 suggests writing, education, media, law, publishing, travel, or language; tied to 2/8/11 suggests finance, assets, shared resources, institutions, or patronage.

Old Chinese-transmission sources give a useful craft shorthand: Saturn for land, farming, stone, construction, old goods, heavy labor; Jupiter for office, law, religion, education, counsel; Mars for military, weapons, cutting, surgery, metal, fire, contests; Sun for authority and official role; Venus for arts, fragrance, wine, adornment, pleasure trades; Mercury for trade, writing, calculation, language, fine craft, exchange; Moon for travel, messengers, public movement, liquids, cultivation, and changeable work. Use these only after the house/ruler structure is clear.

A chart can support several possible professions. Prefer the option that uses the strongest repeated links among 1st/10th, dignified planets, money channels, and the person's era/social background. If the same chart could show sales, teaching, publishing, consulting, or media, do not force one title; describe the range and the conditions that make one form more likely.

## Wealth

Primary houses: 2nd, 8th, 10th, 11th, Lot of Fortune if available.

Judge:

- 2nd lord and planets in 2nd for personal money.
- 5th lord and planets in 5th for speculation, entertainment income, creative risk, gambling-like choices, and investment appetite.
- 8th lord for partner money, client money, debt, inheritance, investment risk, business capital, and other people's resources.
- 4th lord/house for land, real estate, farms, hotels, family property, and fixed assets when linked to money/status houses.
- 10th and 11th for money through career, rank, patrons, companies, groups.
- Jupiter and Venus as natural wealth/support significators only when tied to money houses.
- Malefic pressure on money houses as expense, volatility, debt, scarcity, or risk depending on dignity and reception.
- Lot of Fortune and its ruler for larger material circumstance; a strong 2nd alone may show ordinary money, while large wealth usually repeats through Fortune, 10th, 11th, 8th, 4th, or strong benefic/rank testimony.

Do not equate a good Jupiter with wealth unless it has financial house testimony. Do not equate a bad 2nd house with poverty if 1st/10th/11th are powerful and income can flow through status or institutions.

For investment or business questions, separate the money channel:

- Personal cash: 2nd.
- Speculation and creative ventures: 5th.
- Partner/client/investor capital: 8th.
- Career platform: 10th.
- Market, audience, patron, company, or group gains: 11th.
- Property or fixed assets: 4th.

Astrology can describe risk pattern and timing; do not present it as financial advice.

When timing money, remember that the activated planet may bring income and loss at once. A period lord tied to the 8th can gather other people's resources, investment capital, inheritance, client money, debt, or crisis costs. The quality of the planet and its rescuing/harming testimonies decide whether the person keeps the gain, spends it, loses it, or converts it into status.

## Relationship And Marriage

Primary houses: 7th, 5th, 1st, Venus, Moon/Sun by context.

Judge:

- 7th lord and planets in 7th for spouse/partner and relationship contracts.
- 7th cusp/sign style as a first descriptive layer, then correct it by the 7th lord, 7th planets, aspects, reception, and dignity.
- 5th for romance, pleasure, dating, children as part of love life.
- Interaction between 1st lord and 7th lord/7th planets for harmony or conflict between self and partner.
- Venus for attraction and union; Mars for desire/conflict; Moon for emotional bonding; Sun for spouse themes in many female-client/traditional contexts.
- In traditional male/female spouse-significator language, male charts often inspect Moon and Venus; female charts often inspect Sun and Mars. Use this carefully and adapt to the actual relationship context instead of forcing a modern client into old gender categories.
- Reception between 1st and 7th significators: support, desire, willingness to cooperate.

Separate relationship capacity from event timing. A difficult Venus may show emotional style, but marriage events require 7th-house and timing testimony.

7th-house activation means relationship, partner, contract, or "other person" activation. It is not automatically legal marriage. Marriage is a social and legal form, so require age/cultural context, 1st/7th testimony, 5th or family/status support when relevant, and timing convergence before naming it as marriage.

If the question is compatibility, synastry, breakup, composite, or relationship dynamics, load `synastry-compatibility.md` and read both natal charts before judging the inter-chart contacts.

For third-party or relationship-interference questions, map each noun before judging: the native, the spouse/partner, the outsider, and the act of interference. Use derived houses when needed, but avoid accusation language for living people unless the biography is already known.

## Friendship, Compatibility, And Alliances

Primary points: Sun, Moon, Ascendant, Lot of Fortune, 1st/7th/11th houses, and the rulers of the relevant houses.

For a classical compatibility pass:

- Compare the two charts' Sun, Moon, Ascendant, and Fortune. Same sign, exchange, trine, or sextile gives easier sympathy; square, opposition, aversion, or domination by malefics gives friction or distance.
- Luminary harmony points to choice, affection, and shared life direction. Ascendant harmony points to bodily rhythm, temperament, and living together. Fortune harmony points to material need, circumstance, and practical support.
- Benefics configured to shared points can soften differences; malefics can add pressure, silence, rivalry, obligation, or conflict depending on dignity and reception.
- For business alliances, add 2nd/8th money exchange, 10th public action, 11th networks/patrons, and Mercury for contracts.
- For friendship and community, prioritize 11th, 3rd, Moon, Mercury, and repeated sign/aspect familiarity.

Do not promise relationship permanence from one synastry contact. Compatibility must be read with each natal promise and the real-world form of the relationship.

## Children And Parenting

Primary houses: 5th, 1st, Moon, Jupiter, Venus; for parent-child consultation, also compare child chart and parent chart carefully.

Judge:

- 5th lord and planets in 5th.
- Benefics/malefics affecting 5th lord or 5th house.
- Moon for bodily/emotional rhythms; Jupiter/Venus for fertility/support themes when tied to the 5th.
- For children's education, use 3rd/9th plus Mercury/Jupiter and the 5th.

Some Ptolemaic material also inspects planets in or configured to the 10th/MC region and the 11th-like succeeding place, with Moon/Jupiter/Venus as child-giving when capable and Sun/Mars/Saturn as limiting when they dominate without benefic help. Use this as a secondary testimony; in ordinary consultations the 5th house remains the cleanest first significator.

For minors, avoid fatalistic claims about future marriage, lifespan, crime, or fixed worldly success. Focus on temperament, learning rhythm, parent-child communication, health-support awareness, and development guidance.

For pregnancy or "when is it suitable to try for a baby" questions, combine 5th/5R, 1st/Asc ruler, Moon, Venus/Jupiter, the partner's 7th-house testimony, and timing activation by profection/Firdaria/ZR/Solar Arc/progressed Moon/transits. Keep the answer as symbolic timing and body-support awareness; never present it as medical fertility advice.

## Health

Primary houses: 1st, 6th, 8th, 12th; also Moon, Ascendant ruler, Sun for vitality.

Judge:

- 1st and Ascendant ruler for body constitution.
- 6th for illness and daily health burdens.
- 8th for crisis, fear, surgery, chronic vulnerability, mortality themes.
- 12th for hospitals, isolation, mental burden, hidden suffering.
- Malefics or debilitated planets tied to body/illness houses.
- Sign and planet body correspondences only as symbolic clues, not diagnosis.

Never diagnose disease. Say "this points to a vulnerable area / pressure pattern / need for professional checkup" rather than naming a medical certainty.

Health-risk refinement: a single 1st/8th connection is a warning sign, but the more serious pattern is a cluster around the body/life significator: Ascendant ruler or 1st-house planet tied to the 8th, plus malefic affliction, plus repeated 1st/6th/8th/12th testimony. In live consultation, translate this into health-risk awareness and checkup advice, not death prediction.

When timing health, use the same cluster standard. A progressed, Solar Arc, or transit hit to Ascendant, Ascendant ruler, Moon, 6th lord, 8th lord, or 12th lord can show body pressure only when the natal chart already supports vulnerability. Do not try to name the organ with confidence unless the natal and event evidence are unusually clear.

## Family And Parents

Primary houses: 4th and 10th; natural significators Sun/Saturn for father themes, Moon/Venus for mother themes, adjusted by sect and chart testimony.

Judge:

- 4th/10th lords and occupants.
- Luminary condition and relationship to parental houses.
- Derived houses for specific relatives when needed.
- Reception and aspects between native significators and parent significators.
- Old Chinese-transmission material often checks father through Sun, Saturn, the 4th, 4th lord, and father lot; mother through Moon, Venus, the 10th, 10th lord, and mother lot. Use lots only when the formula and birth time are reliable.

Different traditions assign father/mother differently between 4th and 10th. If testimonies conflict, describe both and let biography or family context calibrate.

## Education, Travel, Writing

Primary houses: 3rd and 9th; Mercury, Jupiter, Moon.

Judge:

- 3rd for basic study, language, writing, siblings, short travel, media, daily movement.
- 9th for higher education, law, religion, publishing, foreign places, teachers.
- Mercury for learning mechanics and communication style.
- Jupiter for higher knowledge, teachers, law, philosophy, academic institutions.
- Mars/Saturn afflicting relevant rulers may show pressure, interruption, conflict, delay, or technical rigor depending on dignity.

For travel and relocation:

- 3rd shows short trips, commuting, local movement, vehicles, siblings/neighbors, and routine communications.
- 9th shows foreign places, long-distance travel, higher education, law, religion, teachers, publishing, and cross-cultural life.
- 4th shows home base, land, family root, and whether relocation changes the foundation of life.
- 12th can show exile, retreat, hidden residence, institutions, or foreign isolation when tied to 3rd/9th/4th.
- Older Ptolemaic rules give special weight to the Moon and the angles for travel: Moon cadent or moving away from angles, Mars configured to luminaries from cadent/setting places, or Fortune tied to travel places can show life away from the birthplace. Benefics make travel easier and useful; malefics make it burdensome, risky, or compulsory.

When 8th or 12th topics connect strongly to the 9th or 3rd, hidden, occult, taboo, hospital, retreat, spiritual, or crisis material can become teaching, publishing, research, translation, counseling, or symbolic systems. Judge whether it is private practice, public doctrine, or institutional teaching by the 10th/11th and the condition of Mercury/Jupiter.

For ACG, relocation charts, Local Space, or choosing a city/country for study, work, love, or migration, load `locational-astrology.md`. Keep real-world constraints such as visa, language, money, safety, job market, and family obligations in the answer.

## Media, Self-Publishing, And Audience Work

Primary houses: 3rd, 9th, 10th, 11th, with Mercury, Moon, Venus, Jupiter, and sometimes 5th-house creativity.

Judge:

- 3rd for writing, short-form content, social posting, daily communication, editing, language, neighborhood/local circulation, and platform mechanics.
- 9th for teaching, doctrine, long-form publishing, courses, law/religion/philosophy, foreign audience, and authority of knowledge.
- 10th for visible professional identity and whether the work becomes a public vocation.
- 11th for followers, readers, fans, communities, patrons, and algorithmic/network support.
- 5th for performance, entertainment, aesthetics, creative expression, romance/pleasure content, and speculative attention.
- 2nd for monetization; 6th for production routine and workload.

For full-time self-media, require stronger 10th/2nd/11th support and favorable timing. For a side project, a good 3rd/5th/9th pattern may be enough. Content style comes from Mercury, the 3rd lord, planets in the 3rd/9th, and the planet ruling the public/career channel.

## Crisis, Crime, And Difficult Topics

Primary houses: 6th, 8th, 12th, malefics, afflicted angles, relevant derived houses.

Use strict evidentiary standards. Historical cases may contain murders, imprisonment, illness, and death, but do not map those labels onto a client from superficial similarity. Speak in terms of risk management, conflict, pressure, or crisis themes unless the user is analyzing a known historical case.

For living people, do not accuse, diagnose, or predict death. For historical/public cases, keep the reading evidence-based and avoid sensationalism.
